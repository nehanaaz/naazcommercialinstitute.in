# Child Poverty in India: Naaz Commercial Institute's Holistic Approach

Child poverty in India affects millions of children, depriving them of basic needs, education, and opportunities for growth. In rural Bihar, where poverty rates are among the highest in India, Naaz Commercial Institute addresses this crisis through comprehensive programs that combine immediate relief with long-term empowerment strategies.

## Understanding Child Poverty in India

Child poverty encompasses material deprivation, lack of access to education, inadequate healthcare, and limited opportunities for development. In India, it manifests in malnutrition, child labor, early marriage, and educational exclusion.

### Dimensions of Child Poverty

- **Economic Poverty**: Lack of basic necessities and income
- **Educational Poverty**: Limited access to quality education
- **Health Poverty**: Poor nutrition and healthcare access
- **Social Poverty**: Exclusion and discrimination
- **Opportunity Poverty**: Lack of skills and future prospects

## Legal and Policy Framework

India has robust frameworks to combat child poverty.

### National Rural Livelihood Mission (NRLM)

This mission aims to:

- Organize rural poor into self-help groups
- Provide skill training and financial services
- Create sustainable livelihood opportunities
- Reduce poverty through economic empowerment

### Integrated Child Development Services (ICDS)

ICDS addresses child poverty through:

- Supplementary nutrition for children
- Health check-ups and immunization
- Early childhood education
- Maternal care and nutrition

### Right to Education Act, 2009

RTE ensures:

- Free education for all children aged 6-14
- Special provisions for children from disadvantaged groups
- Prohibition of discrimination in education
