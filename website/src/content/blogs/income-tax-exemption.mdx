## What Is Income Tax Exemption?

Income tax exemption allows donors to claim deductions on eligible charitable contributions under Indian tax laws.

## Why It Matters

Tax exemptions promote responsible giving and help NGOs sustain programs in education, health, and community development.

Income tax exemptions for donations to NGOs in India are a cornerstone of the country's tax policy, designed to incentivize philanthropy and support charitable activities. Under the Income-tax Act, 1961, several sections provide deductions for contributions made to eligible organizations. The most prominent among these is Section 80G, which allows taxpayers to claim deductions on donations to approved charitable institutions. This provision not only reduces the taxable income of donors but also ensures that funds flow towards socially beneficial causes.

### Understanding Section 80G

Section 80G was introduced to encourage donations to charitable organizations by offering tax benefits. It applies to donations made to institutions that are approved by the Income-tax Department. The approval is granted based on the organization's activities, which must fall under charitable purposes as defined in Section 2(15) of the Income-tax Act. These purposes include relief of the poor, education, medical relief, and advancement of any other object of general public utility.

The deduction under Section 80G varies depending on the type of institution:

- **100% Deduction**: For donations to certain funds like the Prime Minister's National Relief Fund, Chief Minister's Relief Fund, or the National Foundation for Communal Harmony. This means the entire donated amount is deducted from the taxable income.

- **50% Deduction**: For donations to other approved institutions, where 50% of the donated amount is deductible, subject to a limit of 10% of the donor's adjusted gross total income.

For example, if an individual earns ₹10 lakh in a year and donates ₹1 lakh to an approved NGO, they can claim a deduction of ₹50,000 (50% of ₹1 lakh), reducing their taxable income to ₹9.5 lakh. However, the total deduction cannot exceed 10% of the adjusted gross total income.

### Eligibility Criteria for NGOs

To qualify for Section 80G approval, NGOs must:

1. Be registered under Section 12A or Section 12AA of the Income-tax Act.

2. Have their activities aligned with charitable purposes.

3. Maintain proper records and submit annual returns.

4. Not engage in activities that are not charitable, such as those involving profit distribution.

### Other Relevant Sections

Apart from Section 80G, other sections provide exemptions:

- **Section 80GGA**: Offers 100% deduction for donations made for scientific research or rural development. This is particularly useful for contributions towards research and development in education and health.

- **Section 35AC**: Allows 100% deduction for donations to projects approved by the National Committee for Promotion of Social and Economic Welfare.

- **Section 80C**: While not specifically for NGOs, donations to certain funds can be claimed under this section, which has an overall limit of ₹1.5 lakh.

### Process for Claiming Deduction

To claim the deduction, donors must:

1. Obtain a receipt from the NGO at the time of donation.

2. Ensure the receipt includes details like the donor's name, PAN, amount donated, and the NGO's 80G approval number.

3. Declare the donation in the income tax return under the relevant section.

4. For cash donations above ₹2,000, a PAN is required.

It's advisable to consult a chartered accountant to ensure compliance and maximize benefits.

### Challenges and Reforms

Despite the benefits, there are challenges. Some NGOs misuse approvals, leading to scams. The government has introduced measures like mandatory Aadhaar linking and stricter audits to curb this. Additionally, the Finance Act amendments have capped deductions to prevent excessive tax avoidance.

Recent reforms include the introduction of faceless assessments and increased digital compliance, making the process more transparent.
